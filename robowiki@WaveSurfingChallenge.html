Introduction

The WaveSurfingChallenge gives you a way to measure your bot's ability to avoid getting hit by very simple targeting systems in long battles. The WaveSurfingChallenge/FastLearning pits you against the same bots in 35 round battles like the ones in the RoboRumble@Home. You may also want to check out these other movement challenges:

  • WaveSurfingChallenge/FastLearning - Another version of this challenge, with short battles.
  • MovementChallenge - A measure of your bot's performance against a wide variety of bots.
  • MovementChallenge2K6 - An updated version of the MovementChallenge with more advanced challenge bots.
    • WaveSurfingChallenge2K6 - A component of the MovementChallenge2K6, similar to this one but adds a circular targeting bot.

Directions

Here's how you run a WaveSurfingChallenge:

  • Get the reference bots here: https://davidalves.net/robocode/WaveSurfingChallengeBots.jar
  • Bot A fires with HeadOnTargeting, Bot B fires with LinearTargeting. Source code for both bots is included.
  • Run 1000 rounds against each of them, or 30 seasons of 35 round battles for the /FastLearning version of this challenge.
  • Arena size 800 x 600
  • Everything else set to Robocode defaults
  • You are not allowed to fire any bullets

Your bot's score is average amount of life you had at the end of each round. 100 means that the reference bot never hit you. You calculate your score like this: 

 100 - (reference bot's bullet damage score / 1000)
