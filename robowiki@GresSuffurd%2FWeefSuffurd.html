GresSuffurd uses a movement that from a (very very large) distance looks like WaveSurfing. The first version (0.0.3) is largely based upon the WaveSurfingTutorial written by Voidious. It will gradually improve until it is good enough for The2000Club.

version history (newest entry on top)

  • 0.2.2 (20070612)
    • remove wall-segmentation, current absolute velocity is the only segmentation left
  • 0.2.1 (20070607)
    • remove segmentation on distance
    • re-introduce wall-segmentation (5 segments), take preferred heading (without wallsmoothing) iso actual heading to calculate
  • 0.2.0 (20070523)
    • segment also on distance
    • small preference current direction
    • small enhancements virtual waves
  • 0.1.9 (20070317)
    • rollback rolling surfingstats
    • get rid of that multiply-factor for GF1 protection
  • 0.1.8 (20070311)
    • rolling surfingstats: 0.7*old + 0.3*new
  • 0.1.7 (20070207)
    • also use information from onBulletHitBullet?
  • 0.1.6 (20070125)
    • always have a surfable wave (real or simulated)
  • 0.1.5 (20070117)
    • bugfix weighting of waves (sometimes second > first = wrong)
  • 0.1.4 (20070114)
    • remove nearwall segmentation
    • also evaluate second wave
  • 0.1.3 (20070102)
    • segment also on nearwall no - far - near
  • 0.1.2 (20061212)
    • segment on absolute currentvelocity(T-2) 0 - 2 - 6 - 8
  • 0.1.1 (20061127)
    • do not evaluate stop-position when enemy is close (anti-ram)
  • 0.1.0 (20061028)
    • slowing down when wallhit is eminent (but getting more predictable)
    • using proper OnDeath (by David Alves) and similar behaviour at onWin, meaning keep surfing until the air is safe
  • 0.0.8 (20061024)
    • bugfixing stop position evaluation (much more fixing than I thought)
  • 0.0.7 (20061023)
    • add stop-evaluation (Bug: but never actually stop)
  • 0.0.6 (20061019)
    • update 5 bins instead of 3 when hit (still no 'classic' binsmoothing)
    • if direction does not matter, continue same direction (solved strange behaviour near wall)
  • 0.0.5 (20061013)
    • Keep preferred distance, includes very simple dive-in protection
    • Circle around enemy when there is no wave to surf
  • 0.0.4 (20060925)
    • Surf wave until wavefront hits me, then start surfing next wave
    • Use whole battlefield, no more buffer between the wall and my bot.
  • 0.0.3 (20060919)
    • Initial version, largely based on the WaveSurfingTutorial

Checking the details I saw a relatively low score (93%) against bbo.RamboT where I expected a 99%. It turned out that for 3-4 ticks there was no surfable wave, the time it takes a wave to pass my bot. No surfable wave means no movement, which is not good against rambots/close fighters. In movement 0.1.6 (GresSuffurd 0.2.2) this is solved by creating a simulated wave when no surfable wave is available (instead of no wave available). -- GrubbmGait
